Why Quality Assurance Still Matters ... A Lot

June 04, 2025

In an era of AI everything and "move fast" mentalities, it's easy to overlook the steady, quiet role of a QA Engineer. But the simple truth is: Quality doesn't just happen — it’s built, step by step, test by test.

As a QA Engineer, I’m not just clicking buttons or writing test cases. I'm thinking critically about user flows, edge cases, business logic, and risk. I'm that early warning system that spots inconsistencies before they become production fires. I look at systems the way users might, with curiosity, skepticism, and a drive to break things before customers do.

    What I love most about being a QA:

  • ○ Breaking things – Since I was a child, I loved to take things apart to see how and why they were put together and if/how I could make them better.
  • ○ Digging into the "why" behind requirements and the feature to make sure it makes sense for the end user.
  • ○ Thinking like both the end user and the developer: QA acts as the translator bridging the gap and ensuring both sides understand each other’s perspective.

Juvenal (the Roman poet) said “Trust, once lost, could not be easily found. Not in a year, perhaps not even in a lifetime.” This is particularly true in technology and why QA is always needed to assist in delivering products that users can trust.

QA isn’t about perfection. It’s about resilience, predictability, and user confidence.
